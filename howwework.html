THEORY OF CHANGE
Establishment of a prosper is the vison of FAYA. Prosperity is the outcome of sustainable development which refers economic
advancement with social justice, environmental conscience and peaceful and democratic way of life, for today and for the
future. If the basic needs of people are fulfilled with good quality, if, more and more social and economic opportunities are
created and shared equally, if the physical infrastructures are safe and resilient, if the environmental conscience of community
people is promoted, if they practice peace and democratic culture commonly, and finally, if the human resource is equipped
with compatible knowledge, skill and attitude, then the society attains sustainable development and enjoys prosperity.

THEMATIC GOAL:

For making the vision and mission more tangible and h4roviding the objectives of organization more contexts, FAYA has
clustered the aforementioned prospective efforts and initiatives in major five thematic goals. All the thematic goals are
intended to bridge its long-term vision/mission and a set of objectives of organization.

Goal 1: Quality Life Development Objective: Assurance of quality fulfillment of fundamental needs food, cloth, house,
education and health.

Goal 2: Decent Livelihood Development Objectives: Assurance of the development and fair distribution of social and economic
opportunity for improved livelihood.

Goal 3: Productive human and social capital Development Objective: Producing competent human resource and value adding
social relation for productivity.

Goal 4: Civilized Society Development Objective: Guarding freedom, equality, social justice, peace and democratic culture.

Goal 5: Safe and Resilient Community Development Objective: Building safer
and resilient community through safer infrastructure and safety culture promotion.

Goal 6: FAYA a Leading CSO Development Objective: Strengthening organizational capacity, expending its linkage, exploring
new resources and extending its presence promoting FAYA a leading g CSO.

CROSS CUTTING AREAS:

Mainstreaming a cross-cutting issues is a strategy to make the issue/s (an) integral part of the organization design,
implementation, monitoring and evaluation of development policies and programs. For contributing sustainable development
to obtain prosperity, FAYA has identified three cross-cutting issues for this strategic period. All efforts and intervention of FAYA,
during the period, will be designed, implemented, monitored and evaluated considering them. FAYA most relevant cross-
cutting issues are: 1. Gender equality and Social Inclusion 2. Child rights, youth engagement and dignity of aged people and 3.
Risk reduction and environment protection.

COMMITMENT & APPROACH:

FAYA is committed to human rights principles. It will continue Human Right based Approach (HRBA) in its all efforts and
intervention during the strategic period. The human right principle will guide in all phase of the programming process,
including assessment and analysis, program planning and design (including setting goals, objectives and strategies);
implementation, monitoring and evaluation. Rephrasing HRBA in a single quote is to capacitate right-holders to claim their
rights and duty-bearers to meet their obligations.

CORE VALUE:

Transparency: FAYA Nepal is committed to maintaining openness in its operations, ensuring that its processes, decisions, and
use of resources are clear and accessible to stakeholders. This involves regular reporting, open communication channels, and a
commitment to sharing information freely.

Accountability: The organization holds itself responsible for its actions and decisions, taking ownership and providing
explanations for outcomes. This includes a commitment to ethical practices, adherence to laws and regulations, and a
dedication to delivering on promises made to beneficiaries, partners, and donors.

Respect to Diversity: FAYA Nepal values the unique perspectives and contributions of individuals from different backgrounds.
The organization fosters an inclusive environment where diversity in ethnicity, gender, age, religion, and other aspects is not
only accepted but celebrated. This respect for diversity enhances the richness of the community and drives innovative and
holistic approaches to development.

Quality and Excellence: The organization strives for the highest standards in all its endeavors. This commitment to quality
ensures that programs, services, and initiatives are delivered effectively and efficiently, meeting the needs and expectations of
stakeholders. Excellence is pursued through continuous improvement, professional development, and the adoption of best
practices in the field.

Social Harmony: FAYA Nepal is dedicated to promoting peace and cooperation within communities. The organization works to
build and maintain harmonious relationships among different groups, addressing conflicts and fostering a sense of unity and
collaboration. This value underscores the importance of mutual respect, understanding, and shared goals in achieving
sustainable development and social progress.

LOCALIZATION:

Localization in this context refers to three different situations. The first one is the localization of global knowledge and
technologies; Second is the localization of global resources and support as per the necessity and demand of local community;
and the third is the bottom-up collaboration with local community program planning and execution.
